Places to visit in Pathanamthitta

Sabarimala Sri Dharmasastha Temple

Sabarimala, one of the most famous pilgrim centres in India, is situated on the mountain ranges of the Western Ghats and is 72 km from Pathanamthitta town in Kerala. The temple here is dedicated to Sree Ayyappa. Pilgrim season is from November to mid- January. Sabarimala is believed to be the place where Lord Ayyappa meditated after killing the powerful demoness, Mahishi. Located towards the east of Pathanamthitta district in Kerala, the Sabari hills are part of the world famous Periyar Tiger Reserve.

There was many different plant inside this forest like herbs, small streams. Konni Elephant Training Centre is a main attraction of this place. The camp at Konni was one of the major ones in the state. Established in 1942, elephants used to be captured from Mundomuzhi, Mannarappara, and other forests around the area. As soon as a wild elephant fell into a pit, it would be 'outpitted', tamed and trained here by expert mahouts.

Perunthenaruvi waterfall

Located on the Western Ghats of the Sahyadri Range, Perunthenaruvi is famous for the waterfalls there. The waterfalls are known for their wide area, rather than their height. It is located in Pathanamthitta district of Kerala state. The stream later unites with the Pamba River. Perunthenaruvi waterfall, located by the bank of the Pamba River against the backdrop of the Western Ghats, is a popular destination in Kerala for relaxation and rejuvenation. It is truly a treat for the eyes.
